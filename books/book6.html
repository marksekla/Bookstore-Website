The Hobbit

Category: Fantasy

Price: $14.99


Description

Bilbo Baggins is a hobbit who enjoys a comfortable, unambitious life, rarely traveling any farther than his pantry or cellar. But his contentment is disturbed when the wizard Gandalf and a company of dwarves arrive on his doorstep one day to whisk him away on an adventure.


They have launched a plot to raid the treasure hoard guarded by Smaug the Magnificent, a large and very dangerous dragon. Bilbo reluctantly joins their quest, unaware that on his journey to the Lonely Mountain he will encounter both a magic ring and a frightening creature known as Gollum. Written for J.R.R. Tolkien's own children, The Hobbit has sold many millions of copies worldwide and established itself as a modern classic.

About J.R.R. Tolkien

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien CBE FRSL (January 3, 1892 – September 2, 1973) was an English writer, poet, philologist, and academic. He was the author of the high fantasy works The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings. From 1925 to 1945, Tolkien was the Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-Saxon and a Fellow of Pembroke College, both at the University of Oxford. He then moved to Merton College, Oxford, where he was the Merton Professor of English Language and Literature from 1945 to 1959. Tolkien was a close friend of C. S. Lewis, a co-member of the informal literary discussion group The Inklings. Tolkien's Middle-earth fantasy works have been translated into dozens of languages and have inspired countless works of fantasy literature, films, video games, and artwork.
